Why I made a step-by-step Accessibility Guideline for student designers?

While educating myself on the topic as an accessibility newbie, I tried tools with bad-usability, tricked by not free accessibility testing extentions, spent time read long but not-helping blogs. Meanwhile, I found the useful ones, and read resources that I found inspiring. Looking back on my notes, I thought "Why not polish it so that the student design community can use it to promote inclusivity too?"
